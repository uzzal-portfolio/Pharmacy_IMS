\section{Introduction}
The \textbf{Pharmacy Inventory Management System (IMS)} is a comprehensive, web-based application designed to modernize and streamline the daily operations of a pharmacy. In the rapidly evolving healthcare and retail sectors, efficiency and accuracy are paramount. Traditional manual methods of stock keeping and sales recording are prone to human error, time-consuming, and lack real-time visibility.

This system provides a unified platform for managing inventory, processing sales via a Point of Sale (POS) interface, and generating detailed analytical reports. Integrating core business functions into a single interface allows pharmacy administrators and staff to track medicine expiry dates, monitor low stock levels, and manage customer relationships effectively. The application implements a Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) system, ensuring that sensitive data and critical administrative functions, such as user management and procurement, are accessible only to authorized personnel. Built using PHP, MySQL, and Bootstrap, the system ensures a responsive, user-friendly experience across various devices, ultimately aiming to enhance operational productivity and customer service quality.

\section{Motivation}
The primary motivation behind developing the Pharmacy IMS was to address the critical inefficiencies observed in manual pharmacy management.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Loss Reduction}: Manual tracking often leads to expired medicines going unnoticed, resulting in significant financial loss. This system automates expiry tracking to prevent waste.
    \item \textbf{Operational Efficiency}: Searching for medicines in a physical logbook is slow. The digital catalog allows for instant search and retrieval, speeding up the checkout process.
    \item \textbf{Accuracy}: Manual calculations for sales and discounts are error-prone. The automated POS ensures precise billing and financial reporting.
    \item \textbf{Data-Driven Decisions}: Without centralized data, restocking is a guessing game. Detailed sales and inventory reports empower owners to make informed procurement decisions.
\end{enumerate}
By automating these routine tasks, the system frees up pharmacists to focus on their core responsibility: patient care and medication counseling.

\section{Dependencies and Requirements}
\subsection{Software Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Web Server}: Apache HTTP Server (via XAMPP v7.4+ recommended).
    \item \textbf{Database}: MySQL or MariaDB.
    \item \textbf{Language}: PHP 7.4 or higher.
    \item \textbf{Browser}: Modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Hardware Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Processor}: Intel Core i3 or equivalent.
    \item \textbf{RAM}: 4GB Minimum (8GB Recommended).
    \item \textbf{Storage}: 500MB free disk space for application and database.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
The Pharmacy Inventory Management System successfully achieves its goal of digitizing and automating key pharmacy operations. By providing a robust platform for inventory control, sales processing, and reporting, the system minimizes manual errors and enhances operational efficiency. The modular design with Role-Based Access Control ensures security and scalability. Future enhancements could include cloud integration, barcode scanner support, and advanced predictive analytics for stock procurement, further solidifying its value as an essential tool for modern pharmacy management.
